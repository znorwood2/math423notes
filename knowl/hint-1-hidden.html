The poles occur at \(z = 2,3,4,5\text{.}\) For \(z = 2\text{,}\) multiply by \(z-2\) to get
\begin{equation*} g(z) = (z-2)f(z) = \frac{2+z+z^2}{(z-3)(z-4)(z-5)}. \end{equation*}
This function is analytic at \(z = 2\) and satisfies \(g(2) = -4/3\text{,}\) so the pole at \(z=2\) is simple and the residue there is \(-4/3\text{.}\) The others are similar.
