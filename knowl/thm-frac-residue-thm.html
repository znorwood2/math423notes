Theorem 6.2.5. The Fractional Residue Theorem.

Suppose that \(f\) has a simple pole at \(a\text{.}\) For \(\eps \gt 0,\) let \(A_\eps\) be the arc centered at \(a\) of radius \(\eps\) subtending an angle of \(\beta - \alpha\) radians, parametrized by \(a + \eps e^{i\theta},\) \(\theta \in [\alpha,\beta]\text{.}\) In the limit as \(\eps \to 0\text{,}\) we have the following generalization of the Residue Theorem.
\begin{equation*} \lim_{\eps \to 0} \int_{A_\eps} f(z)\,dz = (\beta - \alpha) i \Res_{z = a}f(z) \end{equation*}
(Notice that the case \(\alpha = 0\text{,}\) \(\beta = 2\pi\) follows by applying the Residue Theorem and taking a limit. We will primarily be interested in the case \(\alpha = 0\text{,}\) \(\beta = \pi\text{.}\))

Proof.
