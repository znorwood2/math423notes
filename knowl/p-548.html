Case 2: Only finitely many negative coefficients are nonzero:
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{c_{-k}}{(z-a)^k} + \cdots + \frac{c_{-1}}{z-a} + c_0 + \cdots \end{equation*}
Then \(f\) has a pole of order \(k\) at \(a\text{.}\) (We see this by writing \(f(z) = \frac{1}{(z-a)^k}(c_{-k} + c_{-k+1}(z-a) + \cdots)\text{.}\))
