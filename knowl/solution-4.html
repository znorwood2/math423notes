Solution 5.4.6.1.

Expand \(f\) by partial fractions:
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{z}{(z+i)(z-i)} = \frac{1/2}{z-i} + \frac{1/2}{z+i}. \end{equation*}
Now around \(z = i\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{z-i} + (\text{something analytic at } z=i), \end{equation*}
so \(f\) has a simple pole at \(z = i\) with \(\Res\limits_{z=i}f(z) = 1/2\text{.}\)
The calculation at \(z = -i\) is similiar. There is a simple pole at which the residue is \(1/2\text{.}\)
