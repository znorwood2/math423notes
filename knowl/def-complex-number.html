Definition 1.1.1.

A complex number is just an ordered pair \((x,y)\) of real numbers. But we write \(x+iy\) instead of \((x,y)\text{.}\)
We call \(x\) the real part of \(z\) and denote it by \(x = \Re(z)\text{.}\) We call \(y\) the imaginary part of \(z\) and denote it by \(\Im(z)\text{.}\)
Notice that \(\Re(z)\) and \(\Im(z)\) are real numbers!
We write \(\C\) for the set of complex numbers.
So yes, \(\C\) is just \(\R\times\R\text{.}\) But we think of complex numbers as being more than just points in the plane, and it is useful to have notation to reflect this different mode of thinking.
