Theorem 5.3.1. Laurent's Theorem.

Suppose that \(0 \le r \lt R \lt \infty\) and consider the annulus \(A = \set{ z\in \C }{ r \lt \abs{z - a} \lt R }\text{.}\) Suppose that \(f\colon A \to \C\) is analytic. Then \(f\) has a (unique) convergent series (its Laurent expansion)
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \sum_{-\infty \lt n \lt \infty} c_n (z-a)^n = \cdots \frac{c_{-2}}{(z-a)^2} + \frac{c_{-1}}{z-a} + c_0 + c_1(z-a) + \cdots \end{equation*}
that converges for \(z \in A\text{.}\)
The coefficients \(c_n\) can be computed for \(\rho \in (r,R)\) by the following formula.
\begin{equation*} c_n = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{\abs{z - a} = \rho} \frac{f(z)}{(z-a)^{n+1}}\,dz \end{equation*}
