Remark 5.3.2.

In TheoremÂ 5.3.1, the Laurent series can be decomposed into an analytic part \(\sum_{n\ge 0} c_n (z-a)^n\text{,}\) which converges to an analytic function on \(\abs{z-a} \lt R\text{,}\) and a singular part \(\sum_{k\ge 0} \frac{c_{-k}}{(z-a)^k}\text{,}\) which converges to an analytic function on \(\abs{z-a} \gt r\text{.}\) Their sum is \(f\text{,}\) which is analytic on the common region of convergence of the two series, i.e., \(r \lt \abs{z-a} \lt R\text{.}\)
