It is important to note that the integrand in a contour integral need not be analytic. We can integrate any continuous function of \(z\text{.}\) (In the next few sections, we will see that amazing things happen when the integrand is analytic, though!)
Integrate \(f(z) = \bar z\) over the top half of the circle \(\abs{z-1} = 1\text{,}\) oriented counterclockwise.
The contour is parametrized by \(\gamma(t) = 1 + \cos(t) + i\sin(t)\) for \(t\in [0,\pi]\text{.}\) Plug this in and calculate:
\begin{align*} \int_\gamma \bar z\, dz \amp = \int_{t=0}^\pi (\overline{1 + \cos(t) + i\sin(t)})(-\sin(t) + i \cos(t))\, dt \\ \amp = \int_{t=0}^\pi -\sin(t) + i(\cos(t) + 1)\,dt \\ \amp = \int_{t=0}^\pi -\sin(t)\,dt + i \int_0^\pi (\cos(t) + 1) \, dt \\ \amp = -2 + \pi i \end{align*}
