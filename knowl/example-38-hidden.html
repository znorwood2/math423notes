Compute \(\oint_\gamma \frac{1}{(z^2 + 4)^2}\, dz\text{.}\)
We factor \((z^2 + 4)^2 = (z+2i)^2 (z-2i)^2\) and notice that \(\gamma\) encircles one singularity \(z = 2i\) but not the other. So we define \(f(z) = \frac{1}{(z + 2i)^2}\text{,}\) notice that \(f\) is analytic on \(\gamma\) and its interior, and apply the Cauchy Integral Formula for Derivatives:
\begin{align*} \oint_\gamma \frac{1}{(z^2 + 4)^2}\,dz \amp = \oint_\gamma \frac{f(z)}{(z - 2i)^2}\,dz \\ \amp = 2\pi i f'(2i) \\ \amp = 2\pi i \left[ \frac{-2}{(z + 2i)^3} \right]_{z = 2i} \\ \amp = \frac{4\pi i}{64 i} = \frac{\pi}{16} \end{align*}
