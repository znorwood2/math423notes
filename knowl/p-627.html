Suppose that \(P(x),Q(x)\) are polynomials, \(Q\) has no real roots, and \(\deg Q(x) \ge \deg P(x) + 2\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}\,dx = 2\pi i \sum \Res_{z=a} \frac{P(z)}{Q(z)}, \end{equation*}
where the sum on the right is taken over all poles \(a\) of \(P/Q\) in the upper halfplane.
