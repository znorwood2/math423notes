Suppose that \(\abs{f(z)} \lt M\) on all of \(\C\text{.}\) Then for every circle \(C_R\text{,}\) we have by Theorem 4.6.1
\begin{equation*} \abs{f'(z_0)} \le \frac{M}{R} \end{equation*}
But then \(M\) is fixed, and we could have chosen \(R\) to be as large as we like, so \(f'(z_0) = 0\text{.}\) So Lemma 3.2.3 implies that \(f\) must be constant.
