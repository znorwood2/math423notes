Multiplication by a fixed complex number \(z_0 = re^{i\theta}\) is best thought of in polar coordinates, as the composite of two transformations. Notice that \(z\mapsto rz\text{,}\) for \(r\in\R\text{,}\) just scales every point in the plane by a factor of \(r\text{.}\) And the map \(z\mapsto e^{i\theta}z\text{,}\) for a fixed \(\theta\in\R\text{,}\) rotates the plane counterclockwise by \(\theta\) radians. So the map \(z\mapsto re^{i\theta}z\) is the composite of these two maps: it scales by a factor of \(r\) and rotates by \(\theta\) radians.
