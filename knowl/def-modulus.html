Definition 1.1.8.

The modulus (aka complex absolute value) of a complex number \(z = x+iy\) is defined to be \(\abs{z} = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}\text{,}\) its distance from the origin.
The conjugate of a complex number \(z = x+iy \) is the complex number \(\bar z = x - iy\text{.}\) (In other words, the conjugate is defined by \(\Re(\bar z) = \Re(z)\) and \(\Im(\bar z) = -\Im(z)\text{.}\))
