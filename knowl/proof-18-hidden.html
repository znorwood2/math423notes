Let \(a_0 \in U\) be a point of \(U\) with respect to which \(U\) is star-shaped. For \(w\in U\) define \(F(w) = \int_{[a_0,w]} f(z)\,dz\text{,}\) where \([a_0,w]\) is the straight-line path from \(a_0\) to \(w\text{.}\) (Notice that \([a_0,w]\) lies completely in \(U\text{,}\) since \(U\) is star-shaped wrt \(a_0\text{.}\))
We aim to show that \(F'(w) = f(w)\text{.}\) Since \(U\) is open, there is \(\epsilon \gt 0\) for which \(B_\epsilon(w) \subseteq U\text{.}\) Let \(\Delta w \lt \epsilon\text{.}\) Let \(T\) be the triangle formed by \(a_0, w\text{,}\) and \(w+\Delta w\text{,}\) with boundary oriented counterclockwise. Notice that \(T\) is completely included in \(U\text{,}\) since every point on the line segment \([w,w+\Delta w]\) is (by choice of \(\Delta w\)), and since \(U\) is star-shaped wrt \(a_0\text{.}\) We can therefore apply our assumption to conclude that \(\int_{[a_0,w] + [w,w+\Delta w] + [w+\Delta w,a_0]} f(z)\,dz = 0\text{.}\) On the other hand, this expression also equals \(F(w+\Delta w) - \int_{[w,w + \Delta w]} f(z)\,dz - F(w)\text{,}\) so we rearrange to obtain
\begin{equation*} F(w + \Delta w) - F(w) = \int_{[w + \Delta w,w] f(z)\,dz}. \end{equation*}
What we'd really like to estimate is the difference quotient \(\frac{F(w + \Delta w) - F(w)}{\Delta w}\text{,}\) so we rearrange further.
\begin{equation*} F(w + \Delta w) - F(w) - \Delta w f(w) = \int_{[w+\Delta w,w] (f(z) - f(w))\, dz} \end{equation*}
Now we are in a position to make the final estimate.
\begin{align*} \abs{\frac{F(w + \Delta w) - F(w)}{\Delta w} - f(w)} \amp \le \frac{1}{\abs{\Delta w}} \abs{\int_{[w + \Delta w, w]} (f(z) - f(w)) \, dz} \\ \amp \le \frac{1}{\abs{\Delta w}} \abs{\Delta w} \sup_{[w + \Delta w,w]} \abs{f(z) - f(w)}, \end{align*}
which tends to \(0\) as \(\Delta w\) tends to \(0\text{,}\) since \(f\) is continuous.
