Example 3.3.3.

The exponential function \(f(z) = e^z\) is entire and has nonzero derivative everywhere. The function Log (the principal branch) is an inverse of \(f\) defined on \(\C \wo (-\infty,0]\text{,}\) and it is analytic on its domain by TheoremÂ 3.3.2. And its derivative is given by
\begin{equation*} \Log'(z) = \frac{1}{e^{\Log(z)}} = \frac{1}{z}. \end{equation*}
What's more, other branches of log differ from the principal branch by a constant, so they all have the same derivative!
Warning. Not every multifunction has the property that all its branches have the same derivative, because not all multifunctions have the property that their branches all differ by a constant. Consider arccosine, for example. Or even the square-root, of the exercise.
