Item c.
If \(f\) has a simple pole at \(a\) then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{z\to a} (z-a)f(z) = \Res\limits_{z=a}f(z). \end{equation*}
(That is, the limit exists and equals the residue. The converse is also true: if the limit exists then \(f\) either has a simple pole at \(a\) or is analytic at \(a\text{.}\))
