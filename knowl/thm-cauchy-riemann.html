Theorem 3.2.1. Cauchy–Riemann Equations.

Suppose that \(f = u + iv\) is defined on a domain \(U\subseteq \C\) and that \(z = x + iy \in U\text{.}\) Then \(f\) is (complex-)differentiable at \(z\) if and only if \(u\) and \(v\) (construed as functions \(\R^2 \to \R\)) have continuous partial derivatives that satisfy the following equations, called the Cauchy–Riemann Equations, at \(z\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} u_x \amp = v_y \\ u_y \amp = -v_x \end{align*}
In that case, we have
\begin{equation*} f'(z) = u_x(x,y) + i v_x(x,y) = v_y(x,y) - i u_y(x,y). \end{equation*}
