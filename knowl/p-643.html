Since \(f\) has a simple pole at \(a\text{,}\) its Laurent series expansion near \(a\) looks like this:
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{c_{-1}}{z - a} + c_0 + c_1(z-a) + \cdots \end{equation*}
We simply use the parametrization of the arc and integrate term by term. Most of the terms will go to \(0\) as \(\eps \to 0\text{:}\)
