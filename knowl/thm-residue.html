Theorem 6.1.9. The Residue Theorem.

Suppose that \(f\) is analytic except at a set of isolated singularities. Suppose that \(\gamma\) is a counterclockwise-oriented curve that does not go through any of the singularities of \(f\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \oint_\gamma f(z)\,dz = 2\pi i \sum \Res\limits_{z = a} f(z), \end{equation*}
where the sum is taken over all singularities \(a\) of \(f\) inside \(\gamma\text{.}\)
