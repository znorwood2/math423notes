This is just what you get by applying the Cauchy Integral Formula to \(C_r\text{,}\) the circle of radius \(r\) centered at \(z_0\text{,}\) and parametrizing the circle to compute the integral:
\begin{align*} f(z_0) \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_r} \frac{f(z)}{z-z_0} \,dz \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{f(z+re^{i\theta})}{re^{i\theta}} ire^{i\theta}\,d\theta \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} f(z+re^{i\theta})\,d\theta \end{align*}
This completes the proof.
