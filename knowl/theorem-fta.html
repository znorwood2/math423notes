Theorem 1.1.14. Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.

Every polynomial \(p(z)\) with complex coefficients of degree \(\ge 1\) has a factorization
\begin{equation*} p(z) = c(z-r_1)^{d_1}(z-r_2)^{d_2}\cdots (z-r_k)^{d_k} \end{equation*}
where the roots \(r_j\) are distinct, and \(d_j\ge 1\text{.}\) This factorization is unique up to permuting the factors.
