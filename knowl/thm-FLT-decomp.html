Theorem 3.6.4.

Every FLT can be expressed as a composite of dilations (\(z \mapsto az\)), translations (\(z\mapsto z + b\)) and inversions (\(z\mapsto \frac{1}{z}\)).
“Dilation” is a misnomer, since in the expression \(z \mapsto az\) we allow \(a\) to be any complex number. Remember that multiplication by a fixed complex number \(a\) is really amplification by \(\abs{a}\) followed by rotation by \(\arg(a)\text{.}\)
