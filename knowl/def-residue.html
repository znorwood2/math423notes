Definition 5.4.1.

When \(f\) has an isolated singularity at \(z = a\text{,}\) then the residue of \(f\) at \(a\) is defined to be \(c_{-1}\text{,}\) the coefficient of \(\frac{1}{z-a}\) in the Laurent series of \(f\) defined on \(B_\eps(a)\wo\{a\}\text{.}\) The residue is denoted
\begin{equation*} \Res_{z=a} f(z) = c_{-1}. \end{equation*}
