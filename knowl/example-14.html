Example 2.1.6.

\(\C\) is path-connected! For any \(z,w\in\C\) we can take \(\gamma(t) = tw +(1-t)z\text{,}\) the standard parametrization of the line segment from \(z\) to \(w\text{.}\)
But \(\C\wo\R\) is not path-connected. No continuous path starting in the upper half-plane can reach the lower half-plane without crossing the real axis.
