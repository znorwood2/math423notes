Solution 6.1.12.1.

The poles of \(f\) occur at \(z = 0,\pm i\text{.}\) In light of the Residue Theorem, we just need to compute the residues at each of these poles. We are about to devote a section to computing residues like this, but for the impatient reader here are the answers:
\begin{align*} \oint_{C_1} f(z)\,dz \amp = 0 \text{ (doesn't encircle any singularities)} \\ \oint_{C_2} f(z)\,dz \amp = 2\pi i \Res\limits_{z = i}f(z) = -\pi i \\ \oint_{C_3} f(z)\,dz \amp = 2\pi i (\Res\limits_{z = i}f(z) + \Res\limits_{z = 0}f(z)) = 2\pi i(-\frac12 + 1) \\ \oint_{C_4} f(z)\,dz \amp = 2\pi i(\Res\limits_{z=i}f(z) + \Res\limits_{z = 0}f(z) + \Res\limits_{z = -i}f(z)) = 2\pi i(-\tfrac12 + 1 + -\tfrac12) = 0 \end{align*}
