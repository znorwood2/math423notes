The rest of the course will be devoted to studying functions of a complex variable, that is, functions \(f\colon D\to\C\) where \(D \subseteq \C\text{.}\) Because \(\C\) is inherently 2-dimensional, we cannot reasonably hope to graph a complex-valued function in the way that we did for functions \(\R\to\R\) in calculus. Instead, we think of complex-valued functions geometrically as transformations of the plane. In trying to understand a particular function, we will pay special attention to how familiar geometric shapes like lines and circles are transformed.
