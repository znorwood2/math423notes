If you have seen some linear algebra, it is worth noting that scaling and rotating are linear maps! And you can analyze them using the tools of linear algebra. To do this, we first observe that the standard basis vectors \(\begin {bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end {bmatrix}\) and \(\begin {bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end {bmatrix}\) have (respectively) representations \(1 = 1+0i\) and \(i = 0+1i\) as complex numbers. Since the maps \(z\mapsto rz\) and \(z\mapsto e^{i\theta}z\) are both linear, their standard matrix representations can be found by looking at where they send the standard basis vectors.
