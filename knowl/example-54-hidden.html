Find the Laurent series for \(f(z) = \frac{z+1}{z^3(z^2+1)}\) on \(A = \set{ z\in \C }{\abs{z}\in (0,1)}\text{.}\)
Notice that \(f\) has isolated singularities at \(0\) and \(\pi i\text{.}\) It is analytic on \(A\text{.}\) Here is the Laurent series:
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{1}{z^3} (1+z) (1 - z^2 + z^4 - z^6 + \cdots) = \frac{1}{z^3} + \frac{1}{z^2} - \frac{1}{z} - 1 + z - z^2 - z^3 + \cdots \end{equation*}
