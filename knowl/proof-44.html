Proof of TheoremÂ 5.3.1.

Fix \(z\in A\text{.}\) Find circles \(C_1\) and \(C_3\) so that \(z\) lies in the smaller slit annulus \(C_1 + C_2 - C_3 - C_2\text{,}\) as shown in the figure (TO BE ADDED). Then Cauchy's Integral Formula applies to \(z\) and the closed curve \(C_1 + C_2 - C_3 - C_2\) that encircles it:
\begin{align*} f(z) \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_1 + C_2 - C_3 - C_2} \frac{f(w)}{w - z} \, dw \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_1 - C_3} \frac{f(w)}{w - z}\,dw \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_1} \frac{f(w)}{w - z}\,dw - \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_3} \frac{f(w)}{w-z}\,dw \end{align*}
As in the proof of the Cauchy Integral Formula, we manipulate to get a geometric series:
\begin{equation} \frac{f(w)}{w-z} = \frac{f(w)}{w-a} \cdot \frac{1}{1- \frac{z-a}{w-a}} = \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{f(w) (z-a)^n}{(w-a)^{n+1}}\tag{5.3.1} \end{equation}
holds for \(\abs{z - a} \lt \abs{w-a}\text{,}\) e.g. on \(C_1\text{.}\) And:
\begin{equation} \frac{f(w)}{w-z} = \frac{-f(w)}{z-w} = - \frac{f(w)}{z-a} \cdot \frac{1}{1 - \frac{w-a}{z-a}} = - \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{f(w)(w-a)^{n}}{(z-a)^{n+1}}\tag{5.3.2} \end{equation}
holds for \(\abs{w-a} \lt \abs{z-a}\text{,}\) e.g. on \(C_3\text{.}\)
Put all this together:
\begin{align} \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_1} \frac{f(w)}{w-z}\,dw \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_1} \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{f(w)(z-a)^n}{(w-a)^{n+1}}\, dw \notag\\ \amp = \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{1}{2\pi i} \left( \oint_{C_1} \frac{f(w)}{(w-a)^{n+1}}\,dw \right) (z-a)^n \notag\\ \amp = \sum_{n\ge 0} c_n (z-a)^n \tag{5.3.3} \end{align}
\begin{align} - \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_3} \frac{f(w)}{w-z}\,dw \amp = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{C_3} \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{f(w)(w-a)^n}{(z-a)^{n+1}}\, dw\notag\\ \amp = \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{1}{2\pi i} \left( \oint_{C_3} 3(w)(w-a)^n\,dw \right) \frac{1}{(z-a)^{n+1}}\notag\\ \amp = \sum_{n\ge 0} \frac{c_n}{(z-a)^{n+1}} \tag{5.3.4} \end{align}
The sum, \(f(z)\) is (5.3.3) \(+\) (5.3.4).
Notice that the sum in (5.3.3) converges for \(\abs{z-a} \lt R\text{,}\) and the sum in (5.3.4) converges for \(r \lt \abs{z-a}\text{.}\)
