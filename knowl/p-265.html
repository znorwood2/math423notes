The integral \(\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\) from single-variable calculus is defined over an interval \([a,b]\) of real numbers. An integral of a complex-valued function \(f\colon \C\to\C\) from a complex number \(p\) to another complex number \(q\) will more closely resemble integrals from multivariable calculus, since \(\C\) is inherently two-dimensional! In particular, the definition will depend on what path we choose to take from \(p\) to \(q\text{.}\)
